Wind Turbine

As part of a multidisciplinary engineering team during my freshman year, I contributed to the design and development of a wind turbine for a mock Mars colony. We opted for a Vertical Axis Wind Turbine (VAWT) — better suited to Mars' unpredictable wind directions and more stable at small scale.

I worked on CAD modeling, prototyping, and 3D printing. The turbine used a modular snap-fit design and was successfully tested and integrated into the colony at a project expo, achieving a peak power output of 1.29 μW.

ContextFreshman engineering coursework, UConn
ToolsCAD, 3D Printer, Microcontroller
SkillsVAWT design, prototyping, modular design, cross-disciplinary teamwork, power testing
UAVCAD3D PrintingSolderingElectronics

Drone Building

For my senior year capstone project in high school, I undertook the challenge of designing and building a functional drone from scratch. My goal was to learn more about the design process and how to use CAD to create a working product.

I designed and printed the entire frame, taught myself to solder to connect the electronics, and successfully got it off the ground. The drone flew with both propeller types and was capable of basic maneuvers before a crash broke an arm — a valuable lesson in structural design.

ContextSenior capstone, Newtown High School
ToolsCAD, 3D Printer, Soldering Iron, Flight Controller
SkillsAirframe design, tolerancing, electronics integration, soldering, PID tuning
