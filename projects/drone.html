Drone Building

For my senior year capstone project in high school, I undertook the challenge of designing and building a functional drone from scratch. My goal was to learn more about the design process and how to use CAD to create a working product.

TimelineSenior Year, High School
ToolsCAD, 3D Printer, Soldering Iron, Flight Controller
TypeSenior Capstone Project
CAD 3D Printing Electronics Soldering Flight Systems
01 — Overview

The Project

For my senior year capstone, I designed and built a drone from scratch using CAD modeling and 3D printing. The project was an opportunity to apply the design process end-to-end — from initial concept through fabrication, electronics integration, and test flight.

02 — 3D Modelling & Tolerancing

Designing the Frame

This was my first time using CAD to create a model for 3D printing, which was a valuable lesson in practical design. I had to experiment with tolerancing to ensure all parts fit together and were manufacturable.

A key challenge was a 5 in. × 5 in. build plate constraint on the 3D printer I was using. This required me to be strategic in my design and segment my larger components.
03 — Assembly

Printing & Putting It Together

After printing the frame and buying the electrical components, I started assembling everything.

04 — Soldering

Teaching Myself to Solder

Since the drone involved multiple electrical components, I had to teach myself how to solder to connect everything together. My initial attempts were... not great. But through persistence and a lot of help from YouTube, I dramatically improved my technique. By the time the project was complete, I was confidently creating clean, secure connections.

05 — Results & Takeaways

Key Takeaways

  • Successfully got it off the ground with both types of propellers and was able to make some simple maneuvers.
  • The drone flew for a short period, but eventually crashed, resulting in an arm breaking off.
  • Despite the crash, this project provided valuable learning in CAD & design, fabrication & prototyping, soldering, and problem solving.
  • Given more time, a second iteration would focus on:
    • Stronger Frame Design — reinforce structural integrity to reduce vibrations and prevent breakage during impact
    • Larger Propellers — the ones I used were too small, making it difficult to get off the ground
    • Improved Cable Management — better wiring organization would make the drone more robust and easier to maintain
